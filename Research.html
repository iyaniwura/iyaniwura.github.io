Research Interest
    I am interested in the development and analysis of biological models. My current reseach focus on developing deterministic PDE and ODE models for biological systems. These models are analyzed using the techniques of asymptotic analysis, dynamical systems, strong localized perturbation theory and some numerical techniques such as finite difference method, finite element method, and the closest point method. Below are some of the areas my research focus on presently (with references):

    • Coupled Cell-Bulk PDE-ODE model: Modelling communication between dynamically active signaling compartments.
      Reference: An Asymptotic Analysis of a 2-D Model of Dynamically Active Compartments Coupled by Bulk Diffusion

    • Mean First Passage Time for the capture of a Brownian particle: Numerical computation of mean first passage time for a small periodic moving trap on a refelcting disk.
      Reference: Mean first passage time for a small rotating trap inside a reflective disk

    • Viral dynamics: Study the within host viral dynamics of disease like COVID-19, Hepatitis B, and HIV. Develop mathematical model for the viral dynamics of the disease, analyzed the model and fit it to clinical data.

    • Infectious disease modelling: Development and analysis of mathematical models for infectious diseases such as cholera, malaria, COVID-19, among others.
        COVID-19 PWIAS Working Groups : Mathematical Modelling to Understand COVID-19 Epidemic Dynamics in British Columbia.
